Strftime Helper

Code Meaning Example
%a Abbreviated weekday name Mon
%A Full weekday name Monday
%b Abbreviated month name Jul
%B Full month name July
%C Century number (year/100) as a 2-digit integer 20
%d Day of the month, padded to 2 digits (01-31) 28
%D Equivalent to %m/%d/%y in en-US 07/28/14
%e Day of the month, padded with a leading space for single digit values (1-31) 28
%F Equivalent to %Y-%m-%d (the ISO 8601 date format) 2014-07-28
%h Equivalent to %b (abbreviated month name) Jul
%H The hour (24-hour clock), padded to 2 digits (00-23) 18
%I The hour (12-hour clock), padded to 2 digits (01-12) 06
%j Day of the year, padded to 3 digits (001-366) 209
%k The hour (24-hour clock), padded with a leading space for single digit values (0-23) 18
%l The hour (12-hour clock), padded with a leading space for single digit values (1-12) 6
%m The month, padded to 2 digits (01-12) 07
%n Newline character
%M The minute, padded to 2 digits (00-59) 01
%p "AM" or "PM" PM
%P "am" or "PM" pm
%r Equivalent to %I:%M:%S %p (time in a.m. or p.m. notation) 18:01:36 PM
%R Equivalent to %H:%M (time in 24-hour notation) 18:01
%s N umber of seconds since the Epoch (1970-01-01 00:00:00 +0000), UTC 514440602407
%S The second, padded to 2 digits (00-60) 36
%t Tab character
%T Equivalent to %H:%M:%S(time in 24-hour notation) 18:01:36
%u Weekday, Monday being 1 (1-7) 0
%U Week number of the year, Sunday as the first day of the week, padded to 2 digits (00-53) 30
%w Weekday, Sunday being 0 (0-6) 1
%W Week number of the year, Monday as the first day of the week, padded to 2 digits (00-53) 30
%y The year without the century (00-99) 14
%y The year without the century (00-99) 14
%Y The year with the century 2014
%z The +hhmm or -hhmm numeric timezone (that is, the hour and minute offset from UTC) -0400
%Z The time zone name, replaced with an empty string if it is not found EDT
